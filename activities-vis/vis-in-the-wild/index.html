landmines.education visualization analysis

What is the purpose of this visualization?

The purpose of this visualization is to highlight landmine clean up efforts in Vietnam. This is done by displaying how much area is contaminated and how much has been cleared. It also displays the amount of explosives handled. These cleanup efforts seem to be headed by the government and external organizations.

What is the data? How was the data captured or collected?

Contaminated areas are found using technical surveys. Then further data is collected during mine removal work. When teams clear landmines, they record where it happened, how big the area was, and how many explosives they found. This information is saved in a database managed by Quang Tri province and then shown on the map.

Who are the users this visualization was made for?

I feel that this visualization is made for everyone. Even as we discuss it right we have brought awareness to a topic that we probably didn't even know existed. This further increases the reach of the issue and hopefully earns the organization funds, especially that the visualization serves as direct proof of efforts. The visualization is even accompanied by educational material like different types of landmines.

Questions + Insights: What can people ask and answer using this tool?

By looking at the map and the data shown, users can find answers by exploring different locations and seeing how many explosives were found and removed. This helps people understand where the biggest risks are and how much work has been done to make areas safe again. Donators may even be able to see how their money is being used.

Comment on the visual and interaction design choices

This visualization is stunning and informative at the same time. It follows an amazing design scheme and has modern components without hurting the usability. The map is very interactive with filtering and a timeline feature. It also has all the other features that one expects from a interactive map.
