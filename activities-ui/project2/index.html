Project 2 Documentaion

Project Description:

  • For this Project, we decided to make a smart toaster. Our goal was to create a UI that was straightforward, but maximized the UI to be beneficial to the user. This project put a modern twist on the classic invention of the toaster. We aimed to elevate this appliance that is an integral part of the start to most users' day. We aimed to add features such as a favorites tab to make toasting just that much easier without having to put everything in manually. With just a click of a couple buttons, you could toast at your fingertips. We utilized the phone to make using our smart toaster just that much easier!


Interview Questions

How often do you use a toaster, what do you typically toast?

Person A: I use my toaster for my breakfast regularly, mostly just toasting bread, and bagels. Person B: I use my toaster sometimes whenever I am in the mood to toast bread.

Person C: I use it weekly, and I toast bread and bagels.


What features do you find most important in your current toaster?

Person A: I like toasting different types of bread sometimes so I like having the ability to have a good way to clean the tray that collects the crumbs.

Person B: The most important feature for me is consistently toasting the bread in a specific setting, so it's the same every time.

Person C: Being able to adjust the knob, the sound when it's finished, it toasting evenly, and finally my current toaster allows me to lift the toast up using the same lever that's used to push it down for toast.


Do you have any specific controls or functions that you with your toaster had?

Person A: I wish it had a mode-specific for when I make bagels because currently it toasts both sides of the bagel when I only want the cut side.

Person B: I like how my toaster just pops the bread up when it's done so I don't have to do anything.

Person C: It’s hard to clean my toaster, so make it easier to clean.


How do you currently know when your toast is done? Would you like a visual or audible alert?

Person A: I think an audible alert would be nice

Person B: something audible is nice and what I am used to, but if it were to light up or change colors you can see if it's done even from afar.

Person C: I want an audible alert


What type of food do you typically toast?

Person A: I mostly tend to toast bread and bagels sometimes. My use of one is super basic to be honest.

Person B: English muffins, bagels, bread, poptarts. I tend to use my toaster oven or air fryer more.

Person C: The typical breads and toasts.


What are the most frustrating features you have encountered while using a toaster?


Person A: When the heating is not uniform. Like if the toaster has been used recently, the setting I use normally may burn the bread and that is super annoying.

Person B: I never know what number setting to choose when toasting my food. I wish there was a way to see how much the food is cooked.

Person C: The uneven toasts! I never know when to eject!


How do you currently decide the toasting level (light, medium, dark)? Do you usually adjust it manually, or stick with a default setting?


Person A: I go medium almost always, other people in the household usually change it around but I keep it the same almost always.

Person B: I usually stick with the default setting and then I keep stopping it over and over to see how toasted the food is.

Person C: I tend to go dark.


If your toaster could provide additional feedback (such as visual or audible cues), how would you like it to communicate with you?


Person A: A mix of visual and audio would be good. I want to hear certain things like the bread being done or indication that the toaster is running.

Person B: I wish there were more visual elements cause I determine if my food is ready based on its visual appearance.

Person C: Visual and audible would be good.

This page holds the UI interface for A toaster and its app on a mobile device.


Toaster UI Controls:

  1. Bread Type: Scroll and select your bread type.

  2. Toast Setting: Choose a toast level (1-5) or select from saved favorites.

  3. Timer: Displays the countdown when toasting starts.

  4. Start/Stop Buttons:

    • Start: Begins toasting and starts the timer.

    • Stop: Cancels toasting and timer.


Mobile App Controls:

  1. Timer: Shows remaining time while synchronous to the toaster.

  2. Toaster Status: Indicates if the toaster is running or off.

  3. Bread Type: Shows what bread you have selected.

  4. Favorites: Lists saved toast settings with their Name, Setting, and Timer.

  5. Live Cam: Shows live footage of bread toasting.


    How to Use:

    • To Start Toaster: Select bread, then set your toast level and press Start.

    • Next, check up on the timer via the toaster or the app.

    • To stop the toaster either press stop to halt the process, or let the toast timer finish off for the toast to be finished.

    • Finally, enjoy your Toast!


Future Work


If we had the chance to further work on this project, there could be many other ways the project could go. However, specifically, it would be nice to work on some more stylizing features, for example customizing the background of the toaster to add to personalization. In terms of personalization, there’s much you can consider. For example, adding custom tunes to the toaster and overarching themes would be a good way to get the user excited about the product. We could also create other possible functionality like toasting an “image” onto our toast, which some toasters are able to do. We could further add the ability to have different modes on the toaster that might make the toaster more silent or loud depending on desired effect. There are many other possible directions for the toaster, but we were able to add all the necessities at this moment.
